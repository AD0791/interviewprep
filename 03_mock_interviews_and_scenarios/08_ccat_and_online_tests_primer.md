# The Online Tests: CCAT-Style Cognitive Assessment and Technical MCQs

After the recruiter screen, BairesDev's next gate is automated: a timed cognitive test (historically CCAT-style — the Criteria Cognitive Aptitude Test or an equivalent) and, for some tracks, a technical multiple-choice quiz. Nobody at this stage reads your CV or hears your explanations; a score either clears the bar or it does not. The good news is that these tests reward strategy as much as raw ability, and the strategy can be learned in an evening. Read this after Wednesday's screen unless you finish everything else early — it is next-stage material, not interview-day material.

## What the CCAT Is

The CCAT gives you 50 questions in 15 minutes — eighteen seconds per question on average. Almost nobody finishes all 50, and the test is designed that way; a raw score in the mid-twenties to low-thirties is already a strong result for most roles. Questions mix three families, interleaved and roughly increasing in difficulty: **verbal** (vocabulary, analogies, sentence completion, short logical deductions), **math and logic** (arithmetic, proportions, word problems, number series), and **spatial** (which figure comes next, odd one out, mentally rotated shapes). There is no penalty for wrong answers.

## The Strategy (this matters more than practice volume)

The single most important rule: **never sink time into one question.** The questions are worth identical points, and the test kills candidates who spend ninety seconds proving they can crack a hard number series while six easy questions go unanswered at the end. If a question does not yield within about twenty seconds, pick your best guess and move on — since blanks and wrong answers cost the same, you answer every question you reach.

Play to your strengths in the mix. With your applied-economics background, the math and logic items — percentages, ratios, rates, series — should be your fastest points; take them with confidence. The verbal items are in your third language, so expect them to cost a little more time: when a vocabulary item is unfamiliar, eliminate the two clearly wrong options and guess between the rest rather than deliberating. Spatial items are pure pattern recognition and improve dramatically with an hour of practice, because the same half-dozen transformation patterns (rotation, reflection, alternation, addition/subtraction of elements) recur constantly.

Logistics that quietly decide outcomes: take the test on a desktop with a mouse, not a laptop trackpad under time pressure; keep paper and a pen at hand for arithmetic; take it at your sharpest hour, not at midnight before the deadline; and do at least one full timed practice test first, because the first CCAT anyone takes is largely a lesson in pacing, and you do not want to pay for that lesson with the real attempt.

## A Feel for the Question Types

A verbal analogy looks like: "RELIABLE is to ERRATIC as TRANSPARENT is to …" — you want the opposite relationship, so the answer is a word meaning *opaque* or *secretive*. A typical math item: "A dashboard loads in 12 seconds; after optimization it loads 75% faster. How long does it take now?" — 75% faster means a quarter of the time remains, so 3 seconds. A number series: "3, 6, 12, 24, …" doubles each step, so 48. A typical logic item: "All analysts at the firm know SQL. Some analysts know Python. Therefore…" — the safe conclusion is only that *some people who know SQL also know Python*; anything stronger overreaches, and the test loves punishing overreach. Spatial items cannot be shown in prose, which is exactly why an hour with visual practice material is non-negotiable.

## Where to Practice

Criteria Corp (the CCAT's publisher) offers an official free sample test on its website — do that one first, timed, to calibrate. Free CCAT-style practice sets are widely available (search "free CCAT practice test 50 questions"); paid banks like JobTestPrep exist but are optional — one or two full timed simulations plus a focused hour on spatial patterns captures most of the gain. For the technical MCQ round, if it appears, your existing material is the preparation: the [battle cards](battle_cards/) and the [rapid-fire Q&A bank](03_senior_rapid_fire_qa_bank.md) cover the same Python, SQL, and JavaScript fundamentals these quizzes draw from — the only adjustment is reading every option before answering, because MCQ distractors are built from the *almost*-right answer.

## Interview Angles

There is no interviewer at this stage, so instead of speakable answers, three commitments to yourself: you will take one full timed practice CCAT before the real one; you will skip any question that resists for twenty seconds, without guilt; and you will answer every question you reach, because a guess is free and a blank is a guaranteed zero.
